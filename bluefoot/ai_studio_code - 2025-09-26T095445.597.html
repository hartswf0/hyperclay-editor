Executive Summary & Strategic POML

Executive Take

  • Top near-term “bang-for-buck”: A safari/glamping canvas tent on an engineered deck with a heater and dedicated bathhouse access. Lowest CAPEX path to “true glamping,” quickest to deploy, and easy to iterate.
  • All-season mid-tier: A 24′ yurt on a platform with insulation liner + mini-split; reliable “glamping” experience, durable in High Country shoulder seasons, broad guest appeal.
  • Flagship / beyond-glamping: A 6m geodesic dome with ensuite and view deck/hot-tub; highly Instagrammable and ADR-expanding, but requires stronger engineering and moisture management.

Local rules tilt in your favor (state-level constraints on heavy municipal crackdowns + county occupancy tax clarity), but site-specific building, septic, and fire/life-safety still decide the build details. (North Carolina General Assembly)

Market Scan (Boone / Watauga Lake area)

  • Types & proof of demand nearby: Tiny pods, quonset-style huts, tiny houses and domes/yurts appear in the Boone area listings; these reinforce guest familiarity and pricing headroom for distinctive units. (Airbnb Example)
  • 3rd-party STR pulse: Watauga County has an active STR market with low county-level restrictions per recent aggregator notes (verify locally). (AirRoi Data)

Regulatory & Taxes (What Actually Bites)

  • NC Environment: 2025 NC Senate Bill 291 (first edition) proposes limits on cities restricting STRs. Local rules still apply for safety/permits, but macro-risk is reduced. (NC S291)
  • Watauga County Occupancy Tax: 6% on stays < 90 days; monthly filing. (Watauga County)
  • Avery County (adjacent): Building/inspections under NC State Building Code. (Avery County)
  • Tennessee side (Butler / Johnson Co.): TN’s Short-Term Rental Unit Act governs municipal regulation. Your gating items are septic/graywater, fire separation, platform engineering, wind/snow loads, and bathhouse vs. ensuite approvals. (MTAS)
