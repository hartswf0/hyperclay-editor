Procedural Rhetoric

Stanley & Lehman: Why Greatness Cannot Be Planned

This interface embodies novelty search over objective-driven specification. You cannot plan which combinations will be interesting. Discovery emerges through stepping stones â€” unanticipated intermediates that lead somewhere you couldn't foresee.

The tyranny of plausibility: Pre-specification forecloses exploration. Here, you breed without objectives, follow curiosity, accumulate unexpected forms.

Ingold: Correspondence Not Hylomorphism

Traditional filmmaking assumes hylomorphism: complete vision (form) imposed on passive matter. This breeder demonstrates correspondence â€” form emerges through material engagement, not before it.

You don't arrive with a vision. You discover it by attending to what materials (images, blends, mutations) offer. The cinome grows along paths that continually bifurcate.

Barad: Agential Realism

Each offspring is an apparatus-dependent phenomenon. The blend isn't "out there" waiting to be found â€” it's produced through the intra-action of parent images, blend modes, your selection, and the computational system.

Agential cuts: When you choose to breed, star, or pool an offspring, you're making cuts that produce new phenomena. The genome is entangled with your choices.

The Cinome

A genome of cinema â€” not DNA, but a lineage of visual forms breeding across generations. The double helix visualization shows ancestry, not as a tree but as an intertwined spiral of emergence.

Each generation builds on "interesting" rather than "correct." The pile twists, mutates, corresponds. Greatness, if it arrives, arrives unplanned.
