FUSE Project: Genetic Information Ontology Model

This document outlines the information ontology of the FUSE project using a simplified Olog structure. It serves as both a formal definition and an interactive explorer for the project corpus.

I. Types (Distinctions ‚óª)

These are the fundamental entities or categories of information within the project's genetic metaphor:

  1. File: Represents an individual document or resource, the basic unit of information.
  2. FileID: A unique string identifier for a `‚óª File` (e.g., "ecd1.html").
  3. URL: The Uniform Resource Locator providing access to the content of a `‚óª File`.
  4. Title: The human-readable name or title of a `‚óª File`.
  5. MacroStructureName: A primary, high-level conceptual category (visualized as a "chromosome").
  6. CodonGroupName: A secondary, more specific conceptual category (visualized as a "band").
  7. Note: An optional textual annotation associated with a `‚óª File`.
  8. Keyword: An optional descriptive term or tag.

II. Aspects/Paths (Morphisms ‚Üí)

These define the relationships and mappings between the Types, forming the structure of the information:

  • A `‚óª File` is identified by a `‚óª FileID`.
  • A `‚óª File` is located at a `‚óª URL`.
  • A `‚óª File` is titled as a `‚óª Title`.
  • A `‚óª File` belongs to a `‚óª MacroStructureName`.
  • A `‚óª File` is part of a `‚óª CodonGroupName`.
  • A `‚óª CodonGroupName` is contained within a `‚óª MacroStructureName`.
