LMC 6800 & 7000: DM MS Thesis & Project Timeline (Fall 2023 – Spring 2024)

Course Context & Philosophy

This timeline outlines key milestones and deliverables for students enrolled in LMC 6800 (Project) or LMC 7000 (Thesis) during the Fall 2023 - Spring 2024 academic year. While LMC 7000 is an option, most students complete LMC 6800.

The course aims to provide structure, feedback, and community support for your major MS project. Regular meetings (weekly or biweekly minimum, TBD schedule) with faculty advisors (Richmond Wong, Anne Sullivan, Noura Howell, Michael Nitsche) supplement this structure. Your official MS advisor continues their primary role.

Running Notes Document

A crucial component of managing your project is the Running Notes Document. Each student will maintain this document (e.g., on OneDrive) in collaboration with professors. It serves as a central hub to track:

  • Meeting agendas and discussion notes (faculty, peers)
  • Action items and "To Be Dones"
  • Feedback received and given
  • Progress updates and roadblocks
  • Personal goals and questions
  • Links/references to communication on other channels (email, Teams/Slack)

This document helps maintain project coherence over time. (Link: Running Notes OneDrive Folder - TBD)

Grading Approach (LMC 6800/7000)

These courses often span two terms with different grading schemes. Typically:

  • Fall Term: Graded Pass/Fail (P/F) based on timely completion of deliverables (see points below for guidance).
  • Spring Term: Receives a letter grade, often necessary for the final term.

The final letter grade (Spring) might reflect a combination of the final defense (~40%) and advisor/committee evaluation (~60%), potentially aligning the LMC 6800 Spring grade with this overall evaluation. Specific point values are assigned to Fall deliverables to guide P/F assessment; Spring focuses on iterative progress towards the final submission.

Late Policy: For Fall deliverables with points, the grade may be reduced by 2 points per day late.

Potential New Directions & Ideas

We are exploring ways to enhance the program and community, including:

  • Faculty defining expectations for productive supervision.
  • An alumni "buddy system" for one-off professional contact sessions.
  • Increased team-based work.
  • A DM Hackathon.
  • Recruitment talks by/for 1st/2nd years.
  • Leveraging external resources (UX groups, etc.).
  • Potential move from Teams to Slack for community building (pending discussion with DM club/reps).
  • Industry sponsorships.
