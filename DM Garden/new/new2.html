Guiding Principles & Supporting Structures

Studio Reality: Embracing Flexibility & Iteration

  • No Single Pathway: The program explicitly affirms "There is no single pathway to the defense." The timeline is a scaffold, not a rigid track.
  • Deviation Supported: Individual project logic is expected to deviate ("deviation-amplifying") based on inspiration, research findings, design pivots, faculty mentorship adjustments, and personal interest drift.
  • Versioning is Key: Expect V1 → V2 (or more) iterations across , , and .
  • Capture the Journey: All iterations and artifacts should be captured (e.g., in a with advisor) and combined into the – valuing the process and depth (DM ethos over pure HCI output).

Program & Cohort Logic

  • Institutional Milestones (✅): "Green Boxes" mark non-negotiable submission points.
  • IRB Dependency Chain: T2 Discovery → TSummer Data → T3 Evaluation → T4 Testing/Publication. Delays cascade.
  • Criteria as Anchor: Design Criteria (T3) link Lit Review/Data to the final Defense assessment (T4), providing continuity through pivots ("boundary object").
  • Sprouts & Fruits: (1-Year/Accelerated Track) and (2-Year/Standard Track) are blended in the shared seminar.
  • Peer Mentorship: Fruits mentor Sprouts, sharing experience (e.g., navigating IRBs, showing project examples), harmonizing institutional flow with creative divergence. Fruits may take on legacy roles (critique mentors, peer reviewers).

Feedback, Continuity & Support Systems

  • Feedback System Needed: Acknowledged need to institutionalize feedback loops, moving beyond reflections vanishing. Collect feedback at bureaucratic touchpoints (IRBs, reviews).
  • Distinguishing Feedback: Need to train faculty/staff to parse emotional context vs. reflective critique ("Emotional noise" from stress, jobs, etc.).
  • Alumni Knowledge: Addressing knowledge loss requires structured archiving of past student experiences (IRB templates, project pathways, videos) and reusing feedback across cohorts.
  • Portfolio Focus: Desire for career-readiness support: embedded portfolio reviews, alumni showcases, design critique sessions (HCI rigor + DM creativity), help with narrativizing work ("artifact-to-story translation").
  • Temporal GPS Request: Need for tools (calendars, progress bars) to combat "Temporal uncertainty" and help students track their progress against milestones.
  • Program Continuity: Layering cohorts, archiving pathways, and institutionalizing knowledge are key for evolving the program arc based on structured feedback.

Potential Conflict Zones & Challenges

  • Faculty Fit: Initial advisor mismatch (T1) can cause significant problems.
  • Sprout/Fruit Dynamics: Blending cohorts offers mentorship but can create timeline friction in Project Studio.
  • Term 3 -> Term 4 Handoff: Can feel abrupt ("The Beast"); Design Criteria aim to smooth this transition.
  • Bureaucratic Drag: IRB rejections or delays (especially T3 Eval IRB) consume critical time for T4 prototyping and evaluation.
  • Emotional Noise: Student feedback is often entangled with external stressors.
