Digital Media MS Program Arc
(Sprout & Fruit Timeline)

TERM 1: Orientation & Field Definition (Months 8–12)

🌱Primary Focus: Year 1 / Sprouts

Key Activities & Goals (Aug - Dec)

  • Enter Atlanta context / Begin forming initial project direction (Sprout Intent).
  • Attend DM Talks, meet faculty, assess fit with your Sprout Intent.
  • Identify primary advisor (faculty Farmer).
  • Define field of inquiry + outline direction of travel.

Key Deliverable

  • Required: Submit 1-paragraph summary to Program ✅.
TERM 2: Discovery & Proposal (Months 1–4)

🌱Primary Focus: Year 1 / Sprouts

Key Activities & Goals (Jan - Apr)

  • Conduct literature review.
  • Develop problem statement, research question, and approach.
  • Draft Proposal v1 (5–6 pages) including schedule.
  • Prototype conceptual coherence between research and potential fieldwork.

Key Deliverable

  • Required: Submit IRB Discovery Proposal ✅ (enables summer fieldwork).
SUMMER: Data Collection & Reflection (Months 5–7)

☀️Focus: Both Sprouts & Fruits

Key Activities & Goals (May - Aug)

  • Collect extra data (interviews, probes, workshops, archives) - requires IRB Discovery approval.
  • Engage real-world contexts (optional internship).
  • Reorient project direction based on new insights.
TERM 3: Design Criteria & Prototyping (Months 8–12)

🍇Primary Focus: Year 2 / Fruits

Key Activities & Goals (Sep - Dec)

  • Analyze summer data + literature review.
  • Synthesize findings into design criteria (e.g., “must be unpredictable”) - defines rubric for final defense.
  • Develop technical prototype v1 (feasibility-focused).
  • Initiate studio-led iteration (divergence emerges here).

Key Deliverables

  • Required: Submit IRB Evaluation Proposal ✅ (for Term 4 testing/publishing).
  • Required: Submit Website v1 ✅ (public artifact presence).
TERM 4: Implementation, Eval & Defense (Months 1–5)

🍇Primary Focus: Year 2 / Fruits

Key Activities & Goals (Jan - Apr/May)

  • Implement refined prototype v2.
  • Conduct evaluation of v2 (requires IRB Evaluation approval from T3).
  • Compile Final Documentation (includes lit review, PS, RQ, approach, criteria, results).
  • Defend project using rubric based on own design criteria.
  • Publish results if IRB path is complete.

Key Deliverables

  • Required: Submit Final Video ✅ (pre-defense).
  • Required: Submit Final Documentation ✅ (often combined into the Final Treasure).

Guiding Principles & Supporting Structures

Studio Reality: Flexibility & Iteration

  • No Single Pathway: Affirmed principle; timeline is a scaffold.
  • Deviation Supported: Expect pivots based on studio work, research, mentorship, interest.
  • Versioning is Key: V1 → V2+ expected for Proposals, Prototypes, IRBs.
  • Capture the Journey: Document all iterations in shared folder; combine into Final Treasure (DM process/depth focus).

Program & Cohort Logic (🌱 Sprouts & 🍇 Fruits)

  • Institutional Milestones (✅): Mandatory submission points ("Green Boxes").
  • IRB Dependency Chain: T2 Discovery → TSummer Data → T3 Evaluation → T4 Testing/Publication.
  • Criteria as Anchor: T3 Design Criteria link T2/TSummer work to T4 Defense Rubric, providing continuity.
  • Cohort Blending: Sprouts (Year 1 focus: T1-T2) and Fruits (Year 2 focus: T3-T4) blended in Project Studio.
  • Peer Mentorship: Fruits mentor Sprouts (IRB examples, project insights); Fruits may take legacy roles (reviewers, mentors).

Feedback, Continuity & Support Needs

  • Feedback System: Need to institutionalize feedback loops (at touchpoints like IRB, reviews), store/reuse student/alumni reflections.
  • Distinguishing Feedback: Train staff to parse critique vs. "Emotional noise" (stress, jobs).
  • Alumni Knowledge Loss: Address via structured archiving (templates, pathways, videos).
  • Portfolio Focus: Desire for embedded reviews, alumni showcases, critique sessions, help narrativizing work.
  • Temporal GPS Request: Need tools (calendars, checklists) to combat "Temporal uncertainty."
  • Program Continuity: Evolve arc via feedback, layering cohorts, archiving knowledge.

Potential Conflict Zones & Challenges

  • Faculty Fit (T1): Early mismatch is problematic.
  • Sprout/Fruit Dynamics: Mentorship vs. timeline friction in Studio.
  • T3 → T4 Handoff: Can feel abrupt; Design Criteria aim to bridge this.
  • Bureaucratic Drag: IRB delays impact T4 schedule significantly.
